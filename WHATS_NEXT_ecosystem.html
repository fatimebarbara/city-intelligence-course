City Intelligence for Corporate Leaders

How corporations can read cities structurally — and participate in urban decision-making as genuine partners, not just stakeholders

Urban consultancies Real estate & infrastructure ESG teams

Corporations shape cities more than most urban policy does. But they do so without a structural map — without knowing which gaps their capacity could address, and where their investment would compound with what the city already has. Most corporate engagement with cities takes the form of lobbying on specific regulatory issues, CSR programmes that address symptoms rather than structural gaps, and procurement relationships that are transactional rather than strategic. None of these require understanding how cities actually work. This programme gives corporate leaders the diagnostic framework to read what a city structurally needs, identify where their organisation's capacity intersects with those gaps, and design the governance architecture for genuine urban partnership — the kind that survives political transitions, produces measurable structural outcomes, and creates compounding value for both the city and the organisation.

In development. Contact Dr. Fatime Barbara Hegyi to express interest in cohort participation or in-house delivery.

City Intelligence for University Leadership

How universities can become genuine partners in urban development — not just knowledge providers

Rectors & Vice-Rectors University strategy teams Knowledge transfer offices

Universities occupy a structural position in cities that no other institution holds: long-term institutional anchors with research capacity, talent formation, civic trust, and physical presence embedded across urban space. Unlike corporations, they cannot relocate. Unlike governments, they survive political cycles. That permanence — combined with the diagnostic and analytical capability that a research-intensive institution carries — makes universities potentially the most durable component of a city intelligence system. Yet most universities engage with their cities through fragmented third-mission activities, individual researcher partnerships, and community programmes that address visible needs without connecting to the city's structural priorities. This programme gives university leaders the framework to change that — to read the CII diagnostically, identify where their institution's structural capacity matches their city's structural gaps, and design the governance architecture for university-city partnerships that compound rather than dissipate.

Universities that want to lead urban transformation — not just study it — will find this programme directly applicable to strategy, community engagement, and third mission accountability. Contact to discuss cohort or in-house delivery.

University Module: Urban Intelligence and Governance

Give your postgraduate programme the comparative instrument that no other urban course currently offers

MSc Urban Policy Public Administration Urban Planning

The City Intelligence Index is a structured comparative framework for reading urban structural performance across six pillars — foundational capacity, innovation ecosystems, coordination architecture, ecological resilience, connectivity, and social outcomes. No other postgraduate urban course currently uses an instrument of this specificity to train structural diagnostic thinking. Students leave with the capability to read a city's CII profile analytically, design compounding interventions, and evaluate governance architecture — skills that are directly applicable in municipal government, international organisations, urban consultancies, and research careers. The module is available for licensing as a self-contained unit within MSc Urban Policy, Public Administration, Urban Planning, and related programmes, with adaptation support for specific programme contexts and learning outcomes.

University partnerships welcome. Contact to discuss licensing, co-teaching, or programme integration.

The research behind this course

Book

The Future of Cities

A Data-Driven Approach to Tackling Grand Challenges and Building Sustainable, Resilient Communities

The intellectual foundation for the city intelligence framework developed in this course. Drawing on data analysis and real-world urban examples across Europe, the book examines how place leaders — mayors, urban visionaries, and policy catalysts — can use evidence-based decision-making to tackle sustainability, health, education, security, and urban poverty. It makes the case for data-driven public leadership as the mechanism through which cities can generate the structural change their citizens need.
