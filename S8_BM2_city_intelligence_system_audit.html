How strong is your city intelligence system?

A structured audit of the four components of a city intelligence system — diagnostic, strategy, governance, and learning. Audience-switchable. Output: a system strength profile and the first structural move for your weakest component.

Most cities and organisations have fragments of each component. The system only works when all four are connected. The purpose of this audit is not to score yourself — it is to identify where the weakest link is, so that the first structural move is placed correctly.

Component 01

Diagnostic function

A standing capacity to read the city's CII profile, identify emerging structural gaps, and surface weak signals before they become crises — not as a one-time exercise, but as an ongoing institutional practice.

PolicymakerThe diagnostic function exists when there is a standing team or role with mandate and resources to read the city's structural performance on a regular cycle — and whose findings enter the decision-making process formally, not informally.
ResearcherThe diagnostic function in a research context is the systematic review and tracking of your field's evidence base — a standing process for identifying where the evidence is weak, where structural gaps are emerging in the literature, and where weak signals in the data deserve closer examination.

Component 02

Strategy function

A standing capacity to design compounding interventions, sequence them against the dependency hierarchy, and hold the thread of the sequence across departmental boundaries — not as a project, but as an institutional capability.

PolicymakerThe strategy function exists when there is an entity — a team, a unit, a cross-departmental body — with the mandate and authority to hold the sequence across departments and over time. Not just to design it once, but to protect it as conditions change.
ResearcherThe strategy function in research is the capacity to sequence your research agenda — to design studies that build on each other, to hold the thread of the causal argument across multiple papers or projects, and to protect the structural logic of the research programme from external pressures to pivot.

Component 03

Governance function

The four countermeasures — shared accountability framework, shared outcome metrics, transition-proofing, structural protection — that protect the strategy from the four implementation failure modes. Not a committee: an architecture.

PolicymakerThe governance function is strong when all four countermeasures are in place before the strategy launches. It is partially built when some are designed but not all. It is absent when governance is addressed only after a failure mode has already broken the chain.
ResearcherThe governance function in research is the institutional architecture that protects the study from capture: pre-registration, independent oversight, separation between funder and analyst, and transition protocols for multi-year projects that survive team changes.

Component 04

Learning function

The feedback loops that close the system — the mechanism through which outcomes are read back into the diagnostic function, so that next year's diagnosis is more precise and next year's strategy starts from a richer institutional knowledge base.

PolicymakerThe learning function is absent when evaluations are produced but never formally integrated into the next cycle's diagnosis. It is strong when there is a structured process — triggered at defined intervals — that connects outcome data back to the diagnostic function and updates the strategy logic accordingly.
ResearcherThe learning function is the systematic review process that closes the research cycle — where findings from one study are formally integrated into the design of the next, and where anomalies are tracked as weak signals rather than dismissed as noise.
