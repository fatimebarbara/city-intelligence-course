The city intelligence mindset in leadership · Institutional readiness assessment

How ready is your institution?

The mindset this course has built requires an institutional environment capable of supporting it. This assessment diagnoses how ready your institution is — and identifies the single most important structural move that would improve that readiness.

The goal is not a perfect institution. The goal is an honest map of the terrain you are working in — so that the city intelligence practice you build is calibrated to the actual conditions, not the ideal ones.

Dimension 1 — Diagnostic capacity

Does your institution have a standing capacity to read structural performance data and surface weak signals before they become crises?

Not a one-off study. Not an external evaluation. A standing, recurring practice that produces structural diagnostics — and whose findings formally enter the decision-making process.

PolicymakerIs there a team or role with explicit mandate to diagnose the city's structural performance on a regular cycle? Do their findings appear in budget discussions, not just in reports?
ResearcherDoes your institution have a standing process for tracking emerging gaps in the evidence base — and does that process formally shape research priorities?

Dimension 2 — Tolerance for structural analysis

Does your institution reward practitioners who surface uncomfortable structural findings — or does it systematically marginalise them?

The character demands of city intelligence leadership are only sustainable in an institution where intellectual honesty is valued above reassuring narratives. The incentive structure of the institution shapes what practitioners actually do with the diagnostic mindset.

PolicymakerThink of the last time someone in your organisation presented a structural finding that was politically uncomfortable. What happened to that person's standing, career, or influence as a result?
ResearcherThink of the last time a researcher in your institution or field published findings that challenged the dominant framework. What happened to their funding, citation, or professional standing as a result?

Dimension 3 — Cross-boundary working capacity

Can your institution support strategies that cross departmental or disciplinary boundaries — with shared accountability, shared metrics, and sustained commitment across transitions?

City intelligence strategies are by definition cross-boundary. An institution that cannot hold accountability across boundaries — that fragments mandates, substitutes metrics, and resets at every transition — will systematically undermine the strategies the mindset produces.

PolicymakerName a cross-departmental strategy currently operating in your institution. Does it have shared accountability across departments — or does each department report independently against its own metrics?
ResearcherName a cross-disciplinary research programme in your institution. Does it have a shared primary outcome that all teams are accountable for — or does each team report on its own sub-study independently?

Dimension 4 — Time horizon of accountability

Does your institution's accountability system operate on a time horizon long enough for compounding strategies to show results — or does it reset at every budget cycle or political transition?

City intelligence compounds. But it only compounds on a timeline that institutional accountability systems often cannot sustain. The time horizon of accountability is the most underappreciated structural constraint on city intelligence practice.

PolicymakerWhat is the effective time horizon of your institution's performance accountability? Not the official one — the real one, as experienced by the people making decisions. Is it the budget year? The electoral cycle? The tenure of the current leadership?
ResearcherWhat is the effective time horizon of your research programme's accountability? The grant cycle? The publication cycle? The career stage of the lead researcher? And how does that horizon compare to the time horizon required to answer the research questions city intelligence opens?
