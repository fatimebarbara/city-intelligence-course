Section 2 · Bonus Material 2

The four conditions self-audit

Assess which conditions for city intelligence are present in your own context — as a policymaker or researcher. Select your role to see the questions calibrated to your perspective.

Rate each statement from 1 (strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree) based on your current institutional context. Be honest — the value of this audit is in the accuracy of your self-assessment, not in the score.

Condition 01

Circulation

Information moves across the system — crossing departments, reaching decision-makers before a crisis, flowing between institutions and citizens.

—/ 5 avg

Policymaker context

Think about how data and evidence move within your institution and between your institution and others. Does the right information reach the right people — at the right time?

Researcher context

Think about how your research outputs circulate. Do they reach the institutions and decision-makers who could act on them — or do they stop at publication?

Evidence produced in my institution regularly reaches the people with a mandate to act on it.My research outputs are accessed and used by non-academic audiences within 12 months of publication.
Data from different departments in my institution can be combined and compared without significant barriers.There are clear, functional pathways between my research institution and the policy or governance actors in my field.
When a problem emerges in one part of the system, the relevant information reaches the decision-makers quickly — before it becomes a crisis.Citizens or communities affected by the issues I research have meaningful input into what questions get asked.

Condition 02

Feedback

The system listens to what its actions produce — integrating what it learns into the next cycle rather than starting from zero.

—/ 5 avg

Policymaker context

Think about what happens after a policy or programme is implemented. Does your institution systematically learn from what it produces — or does it move to the next initiative without integrating the lesson?

Researcher context

Think about whether the findings from completed research genuinely influence the design of subsequent work — or whether each study effectively starts from scratch.

Rate each statement

12345
After major policy decisions, my institution systematically reviews whether the intended outcomes were achieved.My research programme is meaningfully shaped by findings from previous work — not just by funding availability or methodological preference.
When a programme underperforms, the learning is documented and affects future decisions — rather than being quietly set aside.The field I work in has effective mechanisms for integrating findings across research groups — preventing duplication and building cumulative knowledge.
Citizens and communities affected by our decisions have a structured way to tell us whether our interventions are actually working.When research I have been involved in has had limited impact, there has been a genuine attempt to understand why — and to adjust the approach.

Condition 03

Alignment

The people who know something are connected to the people who decide something — through deliberate pathways, not accidental proximity.

—/ 5 avg

Policymaker context

Think about whether the evidence that exists — inside and outside your institution — is genuinely connected to the decisions being made. Not by chance. By design.

Researcher context

Think about the relationship between your research agenda and the decision cycles of the institutions you study or seek to inform. Are they aligned — or are they running on parallel, disconnected tracks?

Rate each statement

12345
The researchers, analysts, and experts who hold relevant knowledge are genuinely connected to the decision-making processes in my institution.My research questions are developed in dialogue with the institutions or communities whose decisions they are intended to inform.
Decisions in my institution are rarely made without the relevant evidence being present in the room — in a form that can genuinely influence the outcome.My research outputs arrive at a point in the decision cycle when they can still influence the outcome — not after the decision has already been made.
When different departments or institutions produce conflicting evidence, there is a clear and fair process for resolving it rather than ignoring it.I am aware of which specific decisions my research is most relevant to — and I actively design my communication to reach those decision-makers.

Condition 04

Time horizon

The system protects the long view — making decisions that compound over time rather than sacrificing structural capacity for short-term visibility.

—/ 5 avg

Policymaker context

Think about the time horizons that actually govern decisions in your institution. Are long-term outcomes genuinely protected — or do reporting cycles, mandates, and political visibility consistently shorten the view?

Researcher context

Think about whether your research is designed to capture the long-term dynamics that produce outcomes — or whether funding cycles, publication incentives, and impact metrics are systematically shortening your analytical horizon.

Rate each statement

12345
Long-term investments in my institution — prevention, resilience, trust-building — are genuinely protected from short-term budget pressures.My research design can capture outcomes at the timescale at which they actually occur — not just within the project funding window.
Strategic plans in my institution are genuinely built around long-term outcome timescales — not around mandate lengths or electoral cycles.The metrics used to evaluate my research impact are aligned with the long-term change I am trying to produce — not just with short-term outputs.
My institution measures and tracks outcomes that will only become visible in 5–15 years — not just outputs that are deliverable within a reporting cycle.I actively design research with longitudinal components — tracking the same phenomena over time rather than taking one-off snapshots.
